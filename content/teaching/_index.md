Mathematical Analysis B1 and B2, Mathematical Statistics, and Multivariate Calculus are the courses I have worked as a teaching assistant at the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC). The duty includes grading homework and exams, holding office hours, and giving lectures. For Mathematical Analysis, a TA-led lecture is given every other week at some fixed extracurricular time decided at the beginning of the semester. The lecture aims to help students understand the course materials and solve problems in homework and exams; sometimes, the lecture is also used to introduce some interesting topics beyond the course materials. For Mathematical Statistics, only review sessions are held by TA before the exams. The review session is used to help students review the course materials and solve problems in homework and exams. 

Introduction to Statistics II is the first and only course I have taught as a lecture at the University of Florida (UF). The course is an undergraduate-level course that introduces the basic concepts and methods of statistics following Introduction to Statistics I. The course covers topics including ANOVA, non-parametric methods, linear regression, and logistic regression.
