> **[Editor's Column, Harvard Data Science Review](https://hdsr.mitpress.mit.edu/editorscolumn)**

Each issue of the *Harvard Data Science Review* (HDSR) opens with an **Editor's Column** by Professor Xiao-Li Meng, the journal's founding editor-in-chief and the Whipple V. N. Jones Professor of Statistics at Harvard. HDSR itself is an open-access journal from the Harvard Data Science Initiative, published quarterly by MIT Press, and the column runs with every issue.

Unlike a typical editorial, these columns are substantial, standalone essays. They are as much worth reading for the writing and the way of thinking as for any specific technical content. The recent essays on AI are particularly useful and timely. Also check out Professor Meng's [Google Scholar page](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=4Qr8tRgAAAAJ&hl=en) for more of his recent writings.
